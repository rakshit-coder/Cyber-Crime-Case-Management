{% trans "Cyber Safety Prevention Tips" %}

{% trans "Password Security" %}

  • Use strong passwords with minimum 12 characters including uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special characters
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all important accounts
  • Use different passwords for different accounts - use a password manager
  • Never use personal info like DOB, name, or phone number as passwords
  • Never share passwords via email, SMS, or phone calls

Safe Browsing Practices

  • Always check for HTTPS (padlock icon) before entering sensitive information
  • Verify website URLs carefully - fraudsters use similar-looking domains (e.g., amaz0n.com)
  • Keep your browser and operating system updated
  • Avoid clicking on pop-up ads or downloading from unknown sources
  • Don't use public WiFi for banking or sensitive transactions

Email & SMS Safety

  • Verify sender's email address - official emails come from company domains
  • Hover over links to see actual URL before clicking
  • Never download attachments from unknown senders
  • Ignore emails creating urgency like "Account suspended" or "Act now"
  • Government agencies like Income Tax, TRAI don't threaten via calls/SMS

Device Security

  • Install apps only from official stores (Play Store/App Store)
  • Review app permissions - calculator doesn't need camera access
  • Use screen lock (PIN/Pattern/Biometric) on all devices
  • Never install remote access apps (AnyDesk, TeamViewer) on strangers' request
  • Enable Find My Device feature for remote wipe if stolen

Financial Fraud Prevention

UPI Safety Guidelines

✅ Do's

  • • Verify UPI ID before sending money
  • • Check transaction details carefully
  • • Use UPI PIN only to SEND money
  • • Set daily transaction limits

❌ Don'ts

  • • Never share UPI PIN with anyone
  • • Don't scan unknown QR codes
  • • Never enter PIN to receive money
  • • Don't click "Pay" on collect requests

Fake Bank Calls

Fraudsters pose as bank officials asking for card details, OTP, or CVV to "update KYC" or "block suspicious transaction".

Remember: Banks NEVER ask for OTP, PIN, CVV, or full card number

QR Code Scam

Scammers send QR codes claiming you'll receive money. Scanning and entering PIN actually sends YOUR money to them.

Remember: QR codes are for PAYING, not receiving money

Screen Sharing Fraud

Fraudsters ask to install AnyDesk/TeamViewer to "help" with banking issues, then steal money while watching your screen.

Never: Install remote access apps on strangers' request

Fake Shopping Sites

Fraudulent e-commerce sites with heavy discounts. They take payment but never deliver, or deliver fake products.

Verify: Check reviews, contact info, and use COD when possible

Social Media Safety

Privacy Settings

  • Set profiles to Private instead of Public
  • Review and limit who can see your posts, friends list, and personal info
  • Disable location tagging on posts
  • Regularly review connected third-party apps

What NOT to Share

  • Phone number, home address, workplace location
  • ID cards (Aadhaar, PAN, Passport)
  • Travel plans while you're away
  • Children's school name, routine, or photos with location

Common Social Media Scams

Romance Scam

Fake profiles build relationships then ask for money for "emergencies" or "travel to meet you"

Profile Cloning

Scammers copy your profile and message friends asking for urgent money

Malicious Links

"See who viewed your profile" or viral video links that steal credentials

Stop Fake News & Misinformation

Before Sharing, Verify:

  • • Check the source - is it a credible news outlet?
  • • Look for the same news on multiple trusted sources
  • • Check the date - old news often recirculates
  • • Use fact-checking sites like PIB Fact Check

Women Cyber Safety

  • Use gender-neutral usernames and profile pictures initially
  • Block and report harassers immediately - don't engage
  • Save evidence (screenshots) of harassment before blocking
  • Never share intimate photos online - they can be misused
  • Report cyberstalking and morphed photos to police

Child Online Safety

  • Enable parental controls on devices and apps
  • Keep devices in common areas, not bedrooms
  • Teach children about online stranger danger
  • Monitor gaming platforms - predators target gaming communities
  • Create safe environment for kids to report uncomfortable encounters

Cyber Crimes Against Women & Children (Punishable by Law)

Cyber Stalking

Following, monitoring, or contacting repeatedly online causing fear - Up to 3 years jail (IT Act Sec 354D)

Morphing/Deepfakes

Creating/sharing edited images - Up to 3 years jail & fine (IT Act Sec 66E, 67)

CSAM

Child Sexual Abuse Material - Strict punishment under POCSO Act

Sextortion

Blackmail using intimate content - Multiple sections of IPC apply

Online Harassment

Abusive messages, threats, trolling - Punishable under IT Act & IPC

Doxxing

Publishing private information publicly - Privacy violation, punishable by law
